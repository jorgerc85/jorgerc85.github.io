Thinking Style - How do I roll?

October 5th, 2014 by Jorge Rubio C.

To think about the way I think has been very interesting. Analyzing how I process information, I've come to realize what strategies I use when facing a problem, most of them are part of an automatic process, I don't think about them consciously and I use them as naturally as I get dressed.

I'm mostly an abstract random thinker, this means I work better in environments where I can participate in group activities with people with whom I have good relationships, I don't need very detailed sets of instructions and being in a restrictive environment or having dictatorial or authoritative partners will go against my performance. Do you want to know about tour thinking style? Go here!

Based on this, my experience at DBC can be greatly improved if I do my best at keeping good relationships with other boots, working in groups and trying to set a degree of freedom in my task. From what I've researched about DBC I think that the learning environment there has space for this to happen and it's up to all boots to set how everything evolves during our time there.

As time has passed and we continue going through Phase 0, I've been able to see that the greatest problems I've had come from the way I manage my time, my thinking style seems to be something I can deal with but the way I organize my time, or not, is the main issue I've had to deal with. I've been improving and I've set enough time apart to complete all challenges but I have to get better at time boxing because I keep on losing track of time when trying to complete a given task.

I am certain that I can change my methods and that I can learn how to manage my time in a much better way. I believe that one of the most important things I will learn during my time at DBC will be related to improving the way I think, learn and work. Learning this will benefit me in ways that will go far beyond programming, they will change the way I live.
